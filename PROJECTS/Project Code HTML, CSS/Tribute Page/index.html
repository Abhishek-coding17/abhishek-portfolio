Tata Group

Tata Group Timeline & Key Events

Early Foundations (1868–1937)

1868 - Tata Group is founded by Jamsetji Tata as a trading company.
1874 - Tata establishes Central India Spinning, Weaving, and Manufacturing Company.
1903 - Taj Mahal Palace Hotel opens in Mumbai, India’s first luxury hotel.
1907 - Tata Steel (formerly TISCO) is founded in Jamshedpur.
1911 - Indian Institute of Science (IISc) is established with Tata's support.
1932 - Tata Airlines (later Air India) is founded by J.R.D. Tata.

Expansion under J.R.D. Tata (1938–1991)

1945 - Tata Motors (formerly TELCO) is established to manufacture locomotives.
1952 - Tata launches India's first truck in collaboration with Daimler-Benz.
1968 - Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) is founded, India's first IT services company.
1984 - Tata Tea (now Tata Consumer Products) is launched.

Liberalization & Global Expansion (1991–2024)

1991 - Ratan Tata becomes chairman, leading Tata into globalization.
1998 - Tata Indica, India's first fully indigenous car, is launched.
2000 - Tata acquires Tetley Tea in the UK for $407M.
2004 - Tata Communications acquires Tyco Global Network.
2007 - Tata Steel acquires Corus Group for $12 billion, making it the world’s 5th largest steelmaker.
2008 - Jaguar Land Rover is acquired from Ford for $2.3 billion.
2009 - Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, is launched at ₹1 lakh ($2,500).
2016 - Cyrus Mistry is removed as chairman, and Ratan Tata returns as interim chairman.
2017 - Natarajan Chandrasekaran is appointed chairman of Tata Sons.
2021 - Tata wins the bid to acquire Air India for ₹18,000 crore, bringing it back into the group.
2024 - Tata Consumer Products acquires Ching’s Secret and Smith & Jones for ₹5,100 crore.
